A systematic review of unplanned emergency department visits in oncology identified pain, chemotherapy-related toxicities (e.g., nausea, vomiting, diarrhea), and infections as the leading drivers.15

Strategies to reduce ED utilization include:

  • Proactive symptom management (adequate pain/nausea management and early palliative care referral)16,17
  • Reduce chemotherapy adverse events (dose reduction, delaying the next cycle, using G-CSF)18
  • Hydration and nutritional support (outpatient IV hydration, electrolyte replacement, and dietician referral)19
  • Outpatient acute oncology clinics (refer to day oncology, procedural, or urgent care clinic)5
